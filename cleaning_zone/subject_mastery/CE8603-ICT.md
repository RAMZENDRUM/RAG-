1. Border  irrigation  

> ´ü¢

The  land  is  divided  into  number  of  long  parallel 

strips  called  borders.  These borders  are separated 

by low  ridges.  

> ´ü¢

The  border  strip  has  a uniform  gentle  slope in  the 

direction  of  irrigation.  Each  strip  is  irrigated 

independently  by  turning  the  water  in  the  upper  end.  

> ´ü¢

The  water  spreads  and  flows  down  the  strip  in  a sheet 

confined  by  the  border  ridges. ´ü¢ Advantages 

> 1.

> 3.

efficiencies  are  possible. 

> 4.

Large  irrigation  streams  can  be  efficiently  used. 

> 5.

Adequate  surface  drainage  is  provided  if  outlets  are  available.  

> ´ü¢

> ´ü¢

Drip  or  trickle  irrigation  is  one of  the  latest 

methods  of  irrigation.  It  is  suitable  for  water 

scarcity  and  salt  affected  soils.  

> ´ü¢

Water  is  applied  in the  root  zone  of  the  crop 

Components  

> ´é¿

A drip  irrigation  system  consists  of  a pump  or 

overhead  tank,  main  line,  sub -mains,  laterals and 

emitters. ´ü¢ The  mainline  delivers  water  to  the  sub -mains  and  the  sub -

mains  into  the  laterals.  The  emitters  which  are  attached  to  the 

laterals  distribute  water  for  irrigation.  

> ´ü¢

The  mains,  sub -mains  and  laterals  are  usually  made  of  black  PVC 

(poly  vinyl  chloride)  tubing.  The  emitters  are also  made  of PVC 

material  

> ´ü¢

The  other  components  include  regulator,  filters,  valves,  water 

meter,  fertilizer  application components,  etc., ´ü¢ Advantages  

> 1.

Water  saving  - losses  due  to  deep  percolation,  surface 

runoff  and  transmission  are  avoided . Evaporation 

losses  occurring  in  sprinkler  irrigation  do  not  occur  in 

drip  irrigation . 

> 2.

Uniform  water  distribution  

> 3.

Application  rates  can  be  adjusted  by using  different 

size  of  drippers  

> 4.

Suitable  for  wide  spaced  row  crops,  particularly 

coconut  and  other ´ü¢ Disadvantages  

> 1.

High  initial  cost  

> 2.

Drippers  are susceptible  to  blockage  

> 3.

Interferes  with  farm  operations  and  movement  of 

implements  and  machineries  

> 4.

Frequent  maintenance  

> 5.

Trees  grown  may  develop  shallow  confined  root 

zones  resulting  in  poor  anchorage.  LAYOUT  OF
